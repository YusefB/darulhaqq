### The Sunnī position

Imam Jalāl al-Dīn al-Suyūṭī (d. 911 AH), in *Ḥusn al-Maqṣid fī ʿAmal al-Mawlid*, classes the Mawlid as a *bidʿah ḥasanah* — a praiseworthy innovation — provided it consists of recitation of Qurʾān, narration of the Prophet's ﷺ life, charity, and joy. This classification is endorsed by Imam al-Nawawī's teacher Ibn Diḥyah, by Ibn Ḥajar al-ʿAsqalānī (the muḥaddith of Egypt), and by the dominant fatwa of all four madhāhib.

### What the objection misses

The Salafī objection rests on the claim that any practice unknown to the first three generations is forbidden. But the four madhāhib have always distinguished between *bidʿah ḍalāla* (a religious innovation that contradicts established Sharīʿah) and *bidʿah ḥasanah* (a new occasion or arrangement that gathers people around things already legislated — Qurʾān, ṣalawāt, charity). On the latter category, the printing of the Qurʾān, the codification of ḥadīth in books, the building of madrasas, and tarāwīḥ in congregation under ʿUmar (raḍiya Allāhu ʿanhu) are all answers that were reached the same way the Mawlid was.
