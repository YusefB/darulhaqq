### The Sunnī position

Imam al-Bukhārī's *al-Adab al-Mufrad* records the ḥadīth of the blind man, in which the Prophet ﷺ taught a duʿāʾ that includes "O Allāh, I ask You and I turn to You by Your Prophet Muḥammad" — wording that ʿUthmān ibn Ḥunayf transmitted and used after the Prophet's ﷺ death, as recorded by al-Ṭabarānī. This is the textual base for tawassul. The four madhāhib all permit it; the disagreement, where it exists at all, is on edge cases of wording.

### What the objection misses

The Salafī claim is that calling on the Prophet ﷺ for help is shirk because only Allāh is asked. But the four madhāhib draw a precise line: the One who creates the answer is Allāh alone (*tawḥīd al-rubūbiyya*); the asking through (*biwāsiṭa*) the rank of someone Allāh has honoured is not worship of that person, any more than asking a living friend to make duʿāʾ for you is worship of the friend. Imam al-Subkī's *Shifāʾ al-Saqām* and Ibn Ḥajar al-Haytamī's *al-Jawhar al-Munaẓẓam* work through the categories in detail.
