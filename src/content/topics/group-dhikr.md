### The Sunnī position

Imam al-Nawawī, in *al-Adhkār* and *Sharḥ Muslim*, narrates the consensus that there is no harm in raising the voice with dhikr after the obligatory prayer, citing the ḥadīth of Ibn ʿAbbās in Bukhārī and Muslim that the Companions used to know the prayer had ended by the loud dhikr afterwards. The Ḥanafī, Mālikī, and Shāfiʿī schools all permit collective dhikr; the Ḥanbalī school permits it with the condition that it not disturb others.

### What the objection misses

The Salafī claim rests on the absence of a Prophetic command to gather *for* dhikr in a fixed assembly. But the absence of a command is not a prohibition; the four madhāhib derive permissibility from general texts on the virtue of dhikr (e.g., "the angels surround a gathering of dhikr"). The objection collapses the difference between *what the Prophet ﷺ commanded* and *what fits within the categories he established*.
